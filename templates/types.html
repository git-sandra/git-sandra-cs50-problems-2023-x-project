There are two main types of Clinical Studies: Interventional Studies and Observational Studies. Both may try to learn more about an intervention, which may be a drug, behavior, or medical device. The difference is that observational study participants are not assigned to get an intervention. If there is an intervention, subjects were already using it as part of their regular health care or daily life. In Interventional Studies also known as Clinical Trials, researchers randomly assign participants to interventions. Often, the intervention is investigational, which means it is not approved for doctors to prescribe to people.

The additional way to have access to unregistered drug is the Expanded Access also called compassionate use. This approach is for patients with serious diseases or conditions who cannot participate in a clinical study to gain access to a medical product that has not been approved by regulatory agency. That option allows the use of unauthorised medicine.
